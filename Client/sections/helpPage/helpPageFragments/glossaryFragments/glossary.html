Glossary

In this tab, the definition and meaning of all terms are explained in the form of a Glossary.

Term Definition
Average Energy Consumption View Average Energy Consumption View is a line chart view which represents the avg. energy consumed by the selected carriers for selected iterations as the default graph. The Y axis of this graph is the avg. enrgy value and the X axis is the iteration. Alternatively, the same graph can also display total energy, avg. speed and avg. acceleration over the selected iterations.
Carrier Workpiece carrier (WT) is a part of a transfer system to guide products along an optimal path through the assembly line. These components rest on the two belts of the conveyor, which propels them forward. Guide rails mounted on the side of the belt profile direct the workpiece carriers.
Circles View Circles View is a visual representation of the energy consumption of the carriers wherein each carrier is visualized by a colored circle. The values within the circles are a % of the carriers average energy consumption. The color of the circle can be either red, green or yellow based on its energy consumption.
Contamination Contamination is a change in the physical characteristics of the carriers which can be as a result of external factors such as dust or human intervention or internal factors such as friction. Contamination of a carrier would result in the change in energy consumption for that specific carrier.
Continuous Contamination View Continuous Contamination View is a visual representation of the energy consumption of the carriers which is dedicated for the identification of a contaminated carrier scenario where the energy consumption of a carrier is constantly higher than other carriers. Each carrier is visualized by a colored circle. The color of the circle can be either red, green or yellow based on its energy consumption.
Continuous Energy Consumption View Continuous Energy Consumption View is a line chart view which represents the energy consumed by the selected carriers over a period of time. The Y axis of this graph is the energy value and the X axis is the timestamp.
Conveyor Belt A conveyor belt is the carrying medium of a belt conveyor system (often shortened to belt conveyor). A belt conveyor system is one of many types of conveyor systems. A belt conveyor system consists of two or more pulleys (sometimes referred to as drums), with an endless loop of carrying medium—the conveyor belt—that rotates about them. One or both of the pulleys are powered, moving the belt and the material on the belt forward. The powered pulley is called the drive pulley while the unpowered pulley is called the idler pulley. There are two main industrial classes of belt conveyors; Those in general material handling such as those moving boxes along inside a factory and bulk material handling such as those used to transport large volumes of resources and agricultural materials, such as grain, salt, coal, ore, sand, overburden and more.
Creeping Contamination View Creeping Contamination View is a visual representation of the energy consumption of the carriers which is dedicated for the identification of a contaminated carrier scenario where the energy consumption is continuously increasing. Each carrier is visualized by a colored circle. The color of the circle can be either red, green or yellow based on its energy consumption.
Dashboard Dashboard is a home page for an operator where he/ she has to option to view the system in the form of Circles view or Bar chart view.
Demonstrator Demonstrator is a prototype of the transportation system which is based on Industry 4.0 principles and contains elements like the carriers, linear drives, conveyor belt.
Dimensions Dimensions is a list of views which the user can plot to visualize in the graph. These include energy consumption, position, speed and acceleration.
Flexibility Measure Flexibility Measure is a metric which is a quantitative indicator of the flexibility which the transportation system has to offer. It is the average of the deviation of Carrier speeds from the mean Carrier speeds over all timestamps where more than one carrier is on the Conveyor Belt. In other words: For every time stamp when at least two Carriers are moving at the same time on the Conveyor Belt, their speed deviation is calculated. The Flexibility Measure averages this measure over all Time Stamps. This results in a lower measure, when the carriers are moving equally fast for a longer time, a higher measure, when the carriers are moving at more different speeds more often, and in the measure being zero when only one carrier is selected. The dimension of the measure is the same as the speed of the Carriers. For better visualization the measure is later multiplied by 100 and rounded after the first two digits.
Flexibility View Flexibility View is a page which gives the user an opportunity to see the flexibility which the transportation system has to offer. This page shows the user a comparison of different carriers exhibiting different speeds at different points in time on the conveyor belt.
Help Help is a section in the application which gives the user an overview into the application (project documentation), glossary and a manual on how to operate the app to identify contamination.
Iteration Iteration is a single run of a carrier on the conveyor belt.
Linear Drive A linear actuator creates motion in a straight line, in contrast to the circular motion of a conventional electric motor. Linear actuators are used in machine tools and industrial machinery, in computer peripherals such as disk drives and printers, in valves and dampers, and in many other places where linear motion is required. Hydraulic or pneumatic cylinders inherently produce linear motion. Many other mechanisms are used to generate linear motion from a rotating motor.
Navigation Bar Navigation Bar is a selection menu which appears on the left section of the product. This menu allows the user the option to direct reach the following pages: Home, Circles View, Bar Chart View, Flexibility View, Simulation, Help.
Session Session is a running instance of the selected data file.
Simulation View Simulation View is a page which allows the user to upload/ select the data files which they would like to analyze and specify the relevant parameters for that file. This feature allows the user to emulate the real time data generation.
Spike View Spike View is a page which allows the user to identify contamination scenarios wherein there has been a sudden energy surge because of an external interference. This view provides a table to the user which lists the carrier and the iterations where energy surges have been observed. It also gives the opportunity to the user to directly reach the continuous energy consumption graph for this selected carrier and iteration.
