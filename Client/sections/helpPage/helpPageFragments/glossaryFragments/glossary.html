Glossary

In this tab, the definition and meaning of all terms are explained in the form of a Glossary.

Term Definition
Linear Drive A linear actuator creates motion in a straight line, in contrast to the circular motion of a conventional electric motor. Linear actuators are used in machine tools and industrial machinery, in computer peripherals such as disk drives and printers, in valves and dampers, and in many other places where linear motion is required. Hydraulic or pneumatic cylinders inherently produce linear motion. Many other mechanisms are used to generate linear motion from a rotating motor.
Carrier Workpiece carrier (WT) is a part of a transfer system to guide products along an optimal path through the assembly line. These components rest on the two belts of the conveyor, which propels them forward. Guide rails mounted on the side of the belt profile direct the workpiece carriers.
Conveyor Belt A conveyor belt is the carrying medium of a belt conveyor system (often shortened to belt conveyor). A belt conveyor system is one of many types of conveyor systems. A belt conveyor system consists of two or more pulleys (sometimes referred to as drums), with an endless loop of carrying medium—the conveyor belt—that rotates about them. One or both of the pulleys are powered, moving the belt and the material on the belt forward. The powered pulley is called the drive pulley while the unpowered pulley is called the idler pulley. There are two main industrial classes of belt conveyors; Those in general material handling such as those moving boxes along inside a factory and bulk material handling such as those used to transport large volumes of resources and agricultural materials, such as grain, salt, coal, ore, sand, overburden and more.
Contamination Contanimation is a change in the physical characteristics of the carriers which can be as a result of external factors such as dust or human intervention or internal factors such as friction. Contanimation of a carrier would result in the change in energy consumption for that specific carrier.
Demonstrator Demonstrator is a prototype of the transposrtation system which is based on Industry 4.0 principles and contains elements like the carriers, linear drives, converyor belt.
Iteration Iteration is a single run of a carrier on the conveyor belt.
Circles View Contanimation is a change in the physical characteristics of the carriers which can be as a result of external factors such as dust or human intervention or internal factors such as friction. Contanimation of a carrier would result in the change in energy consumption for that specific carrier.
Graph View Graph View is a home page for an operator where he/ she has to option to view the system in the form of Circles view or Bar chart view.
Comparison View Comparison View is a line chart representing the energy consumption of different carriers with respect to each other over a period of defined time
Comparison Pane Contanimation is a change in the physical characteristics of the carriers which can be as a result of external factors such as dust or human intervention or internal factors such as friction. Contanimation of a carrier would result in the change in energy consumption for that specific carrier.
Drill Down Chart Drill Down Chart is a line chart which represents the energy consumed by a particular carrier over multiple iterations.
Continuous Energy Consumption View Continuous Energy Consumption View is a line chart view which represents the energy consumed by the selected carriers over a period of time. The Y axis of this graph is the enrgy value and the X axis is the timestamp.
Average Energy Consumption View Average Energy Consumption View is a line chart view which represents the avg. energy consumed by the selected carriers for selected iterations. The Y axis of this graph is the avg. enrgy value and the X axis is the iteration. Alternatively, the same graph can also display avg. speed and avg. acceleration over the selected iterations.
Flexibility Measure The Flexibility Measure is the average of the deviation of Carrier speeds from the mean Carrier speeds over all timestamps where more than one carrier is on the Conveyor Belt. In other words: For every time stamp when at least two Carriers are moving at the same time on the Conveyor Belt, their speed deviation is calculated. The Flexibility Measure averages this measure over all Time Stamps. This results in a lower measure, when the carriers are moving equally fast for a longer time, a higher measure, when the carriers are moving at more different speeds more often, and in the measure being zero when only one carrier is selected. The dimension of the measure is the same as the speed of the Carriers. For better visualization the measure is later multiplied by 100 and rounded after the first two digits.
